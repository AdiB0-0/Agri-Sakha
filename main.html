What is Agriculture?

Agriculture is the fundamental practice of cultivating plants and raising livestock. It represents the cornerstone of human civilization, providing the vast majority of the world's food, fabrics, and biofuels. From ancient farming techniques to modern precision technology, agriculture encompasses a wide range of sciences and arts dedicated to land stewardship, crop production, and animal husbandry to sustain and enhance human life.

The Details of Agriculture

Core Practices

At its heart, agriculture is the art and science of cultivating the soil, growing crops, and raising livestock. It includes the preparation of plant and animal products for people to use and their distribution to markets.

A Brief History

The history of agriculture began thousands of years ago. The Fertile Crescent of Western Asia, Egypt, and India were sites of the earliest planned sowing and harvesting of plants. The Agricultural Revolution later introduced scientific principles and new technologies that drastically increased crop yields.

Modern Agricultural Practices

Today, agriculture has evolved into a complex system incorporating advanced technology like Precision Agriculture, Biotechnology, Organic Farming, and Vertical Farming to enhance efficiency and sustainability.
